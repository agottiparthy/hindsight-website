## The honest comparison

Both Hindsight and Clozd help teams understand why they win and lose deals. Both have platforms. But they are built on fundamentally different models. The right choice depends on how much of your pipeline you actually want to understand.

The Deal Review Agent investigates every closed deal within 48 hours of close. It cross-references CRM data, call transcripts, and email threads. **No sampling. No per-interview fees. No waiting 90 days.**

## Clozd

### Managed win-loss research with a reporting layer

Clozd runs your win-loss program for you. Their analysts conduct interviews, synthesize findings, and present insights to your leadership team. They have a platform to view and report on that data.

If you want to fully outsource win-loss and have a team manage the interviews and present findings to your C-suite, Clozd does that well.

The tradeoffs: interviews are priced individually, which naturally limits how many you run. Coverage stays low by design. Insights take 60 to 90 days to arrive. The platform is a reporting layer on top of that sampled dataset. It is not built on automated deal analysis.

### Best for

Teams without a dedicated win-loss owner internally. Companies that want occasional deep-dive research presented to leadership. Organizations where analyst-curated interview quality matters more than coverage and speed.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can Hindsight replace a managed win-loss program like Clozd?

Yes, for most in-house teams. Hindsight automates the analysis and interviews that Clozd does manually. The difference is coverage, speed, and cost. Hindsight analyzes 100% of deals in real time with interviews included. Clozd covers a sample over several months with per-interview pricing. If you want fully outsourced research with no internal platform ownership, a managed program may still be the right fit.

### How does Hindsight's interview quality compare to Clozd?

Clozd uses human analysts to conduct interviews, which produces high depth for individually selected deals. Hindsight uses AI-powered interviews personalized to each deal, sent within 48 hours of close. The difference is scale versus depth. Hindsight produces dramatically higher volume across every deal. Clozd produces carefully curated interviews on a small sample. Most teams find that breadth across 100% of deals surfaces patterns that a sample never would.

### Why does Clozd's per-interview pricing matter?

When every interview has a line item cost, teams naturally limit volume to control spend. That is why most managed programs cover less than 5% of deals. The program stays thin by design. Hindsight includes interviews automatically on every deal. There is no volume decision to make.

### Both have platforms. What is the difference?

Clozd's platform is a reporting layer built on analyst-curated interview data from a sample of deals. Hindsight's platform is built on automated analysis of every closed deal, cross-referenced across CRM, calls, emails, and buyer interviews. The interface is similar. The data underneath is fundamentally different.

### Does Hindsight have an MCP like Clozd?

Yes. Hindsight's MCP connects Claude, Glean, and other AI tools to verified deal records from 100% of your pipeline. The key difference is what those tools are reading. Hindsight's MCP feeds agents verified, cross-referenced deal intelligence. The quality of the underlying data determines the quality of every AI output built on top of it.

### Is Hindsight more affordable than Clozd?

Yes. Clozd's managed programs typically run $50K to $100K or more per project, plus per-interview fees. Hindsight is a platform subscription that covers your entire pipeline on an ongoing basis at a fraction of that cost.
